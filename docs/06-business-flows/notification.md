# Notification Business Flow

> Sports Venue Management System (SVMS)

---

# Purpose

This document describes how SVMS delivers operational communications to Customers and Businesses.

Unlike Booking, Payment, or Review, Notification does not initiate or control business processes.

Its responsibility is to communicate important business events to the appropriate recipients through supported communication channels.

---

# Business Goal

The goal of the Notification domain is to:

- Keep Customers informed throughout their booking journey.
- Keep Businesses informed about operational activities.
- Reduce missed bookings and operational mistakes.
- Deliver timely and reliable communications.
- Support business operations without affecting business transactions.

At the end of this process:

- Relevant parties receive important updates.
- Communication history is available.
- Business processes continue regardless of notification delivery status.

---

# Notification Definition

A Notification is a communication generated in response to a business event.

Notifications are event-driven.

Notifications never initiate business operations.

Notifications never become the source of truth for operational data.

---

# Primary Participants

## Customer

Receives notifications related to:

- Booking
- Payment
- Review
- Account

---

## Business

Receives notifications related to:

- New Bookings
- Payments
- Reviews
- Subscription
- Operational activities

Business Members receive notifications according to their assigned permissions.

---

# Supporting Participant

## SVMS Platform

Responsible for:

- Detecting business events
- Creating notifications
- Delivering notifications
- Retrying failed deliveries
- Recording notification history

---

# Preconditions

Before a Notification can be created:

- A valid business event has occurred.
- The recipient has been identified.
- The selected delivery channel is available.

# Delivery Lifecycle

```text
Queued
        │
        ▼
Sending
        │
        ├──────────────┐
        ▼              ▼
Delivered       Failed
```

Failed deliveries may be retried automatically.

Delivery lifecycle is independent from business processes.

# Business Steps

## Step 1 — Business Event Occurs

A business event is completed.

Examples:

- Booking Confirmed
- Payment Paid
- Review Submitted
- Trial Expiring

Expected Result:

SVMS determines whether a Notification should be created.

---

## Step 2 — Create Notification

SVMS creates a Notification.

Expected Result:

A Notification enters the delivery queue.

---

## Step 3 — Select Delivery Channel

SVMS determines which communication channels should be used.

Expected Result:

Appropriate delivery channels are selected.

---

## Step 4 — Deliver Notification

SVMS attempts to deliver the Notification.

Expected Result:

Notification reaches the intended recipient.

---

## Step 5 — Retry Failed Delivery

If delivery fails, SVMS retries automatically according to the configured retry policy.

Expected Result:

Temporary delivery failures are recovered automatically.

---

## Step 6 — Record Notification History

Delivered Notifications are stored in the Notification Center.

Expected Result:

Recipients can review recent notification history.

---

# Notification Center

SVMS provides a Notification Center for both Customers and Businesses.

Examples:

Customer:

- Booking Confirmed
- Booking Reminder
- Payment Successful
- Review Available

Business:

- New Booking
- Customer Checked In
- New Review
- Trial Expiring

Notification Center is intended for operational awareness only.

Operational data remains within its respective business domain.

---

# Notification Preferences

Operational Notifications cannot be disabled.

Examples:

- Booking Confirmation
- Booking Cancellation
- Payment Confirmation

Optional Notifications may be disabled.

Examples:

- Marketing Campaigns
- Product Announcements
- Feature Updates

---

# Booking Reminder

The MVP uses fixed reminder schedules.

Customers receive reminders:

- 24 hours before booking.
- 2 hours before booking.

Reminder schedules are not configurable in the MVP.

---

# Subscription Reminder

To avoid unnecessary spam, Subscription reminders are sent only for significant events.

Examples:

- Trial expires in 7 days
- Trial expires in 3 days
- Trial expires in 1 day
- Subscription expired

---

# Notification Retention

Notifications are retained in the Notification Center for 90 days.

Notification history is intended for operational reference only.

Historical business records remain available within their respective domains.

---

# Business Rules

### BR-001

Notifications are generated only after a business event occurs.

---

### BR-002

Notifications must never change business state.

---

### BR-003

Notification delivery failures must not interrupt business processes.

---

### BR-004

Operational Notifications cannot be disabled.

---

### BR-005

Marketing Notifications may be disabled by recipients.

---

### BR-006

Business Members receive Notifications according to their assigned permissions.

---

### BR-007

Notification delivery failures may be retried automatically.

---

### BR-008

Notification Center retains Notifications for 90 days.

---

### BR-009

Notification history is not the source of truth for business records.

---

### BR-010

Only supported delivery channels may be used.

---

# Alternative Flows

## AF-001

Notification delivery fails.

Result:

SVMS retries delivery automatically.

---

## AF-002

Recipient has disabled optional Notifications.

Result:

Only operational Notifications are delivered.

---

## AF-003

Multiple Notifications are generated from different business events.

Result:

Each Notification is processed independently.

---

# Failure Flows

## FF-001

Email service is temporarily unavailable.

Result:

Notification remains in the retry queue.

---

## FF-002

Recipient contact information is invalid.

Result:

Delivery fails while business operations continue normally.

---

## FF-003

Notification channel is unavailable.

Result:

Other available channels may still be used.

---

# Completion Criteria

The Notification process is considered complete when:

- Delivery succeeds.

OR

- All retry attempts have been exhausted.

Business operations remain unaffected regardless of the delivery outcome.

---

# Success Metrics

The Notification process succeeds when:

- Operational Notifications are delivered reliably.
- Customers receive timely booking reminders.
- Businesses stay informed about important operational events.
- Notification failures do not interrupt business operations.
- Notification Center provides useful operational history.

# Design Principles

The Notification domain follows these principles:

- Notifications communicate business events.
- Notifications never control business processes.
- Delivery failures never interrupt business operations.
- Notification history is not the source of truth.
- Operational communications take priority over marketing communications.
