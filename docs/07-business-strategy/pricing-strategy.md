# Pricing Strategy

> Sports Venue Management System (SVMS)

---

# Purpose

This document defines the monetization strategy of SVMS.

Unlike Business Flow documents, which describe how the platform operates, this document explains how SVMS generates sustainable revenue while aligning its success with the growth of its customers.

Pricing is not only about setting prices.

It is a strategic tool that balances customer success, platform sustainability, and long-term business growth.

---

# Business Goal

The pricing strategy aims to:

- Keep the barrier to entry low for new Businesses.
- Allow Businesses to experience real value before purchasing.
- Align SVMS revenue with Business growth.
- Build predictable recurring revenue.
- Create long-term incentives for Businesses to upgrade.

At the end of this strategy:

- Businesses understand what they are paying for.
- Pricing remains simple and transparent.
- Revenue grows alongside customer success.

---

# Pricing Philosophy

SVMS follows several pricing principles.

## Customer Success First

Businesses should experience measurable value before paying for the platform.

---

## Grow Together

As Businesses grow, SVMS grows together with them.

Revenue should align with customer success rather than compete against it.

---

## Simplicity Wins

Pricing should be easy to understand.

Businesses should immediately recognize the value of upgrading.

---

## Feature-Based Pricing

Subscription plans differentiate platform capabilities rather than storage limits.

SVMS sells business capabilities—not data limits.

---

## Fair Monetization

Pricing should never punish successful Businesses.

Higher subscription plans reduce transaction fees to reward business growth.

---

# Revenue Model

SVMS adopts a hybrid monetization strategy.

```text
Business
        │
        ├── Subscription
        │
        └── Transaction Fee
                │
                ▼
        SVMS Revenue
```

The hybrid model combines:

- Predictable recurring revenue through Subscription.
- Scalable revenue through Transaction Fees.

This aligns SVMS revenue with Business growth while maintaining stable platform income.

# Free Trial Strategy

Every new Business receives a Free Trial.

The Free Trial begins after Business Onboarding has been successfully completed.

The Free Trial ends when:

- 14 calendar days have passed

OR

- The Business completes 10 Bookings

whichever comes first.

The goal is to allow Businesses to experience real customer activity before purchasing a subscription.

---

# Trial Experience

During the Free Trial, Businesses receive access to nearly all Growth Plan capabilities.

The objective is to allow Businesses to fully experience the platform before making a purchasing decision.

Artificial feature restrictions during the Trial should be minimized.

---


# Feature Gating

Subscription plans are differentiated by business capabilities.

Examples include:

- Analytics
- Advanced Pricing
- Staff Management
- Promotions
- AI Features
- Multi-Branch Management

The exact feature matrix is maintained separately from this document to allow future evolution without changing the pricing philosophy.

---

# Transaction Fee Strategy

SVMS applies transaction fees in addition to Subscription.

Transaction fees encourage long-term alignment between platform growth and Business success.

As subscription plans increase, transaction fees decrease.

Example:

| Plan | Transaction Fee |
|------|----------------:|
| Starter | Higher |
| Growth | Lower |
| Enterprise (Future) | Optional / 0% |

Exact percentages are intentionally excluded from this document and may evolve over time.

---

# Platform Fee Responsibility

During the MVP:

Businesses absorb transaction fees.

Customers pay only the displayed booking price.

This keeps the booking experience simple while reducing friction during checkout.

Future versions may allow Businesses to choose whether platform fees are absorbed by the Business or passed to Customers.

---

# Billing Strategy

The MVP supports:

- Monthly Billing

Future versions may introduce:

- Annual Billing
- Quarterly Billing
- Multi-Year Contracts

---

# Upgrade Strategy

Businesses may upgrade their Subscription at any time.

Upgrade benefits become available immediately.

The objective is to allow Businesses to unlock value without unnecessary waiting periods.

---

# Downgrade Strategy

Businesses may downgrade their Subscription.

When downgrading:

- Historical data is preserved.
- Locked capabilities become unavailable.
- Business operations should not be disrupted unexpectedly.

A Grace Period may be provided to allow Businesses time to adjust before downgrade restrictions are fully enforced.

---

# Subscription Expiration

When a Subscription expires:

- Historical business data remains available.
- Businesses retain read-only access.
- New operational activities become restricted according to Subscription rules.

Businesses never lose ownership of their historical data because of subscription changes.

---

# Trial Abuse Prevention

SVMS should protect the platform against repeated Free Trial abuse.

Possible verification methods may include:

- Email Verification
- Phone Verification
- Business Verification
- Device Signals
- Manual Review

The implementation may evolve over time while preserving a smooth onboarding experience for legitimate Businesses.

# Design Principles

The Pricing Strategy follows these principles:

- Businesses should pay because they receive value.
- Pricing should encourage growth—not create barriers.
- Revenue grows together with Business success.
- Historical data belongs to the Business.
- Simplicity is preferred over commercial complexity.
- Trust is more valuable than short-term revenue.
- Long-term customer relationships are prioritized over aggressive monetization.
